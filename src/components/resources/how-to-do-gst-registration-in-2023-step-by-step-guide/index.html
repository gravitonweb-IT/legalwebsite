How to do GST Registration in 2023 – Step by Step Guide

GST Guide - Guide
  • Update on August 18, 2023

In 2023, the process of registering for Goods and Services Tax (GST) has become more streamlined and user-friendly, thanks to the GST portal’s advancements. Whether you are a sole proprietor, a service provider, or an e-commerce entity, understanding the GST registration procedure is crucial to ensuring compliance with the taxation system. In this guide, we will break down the process into simple steps, using relevant keywords, to help you navigate through the GST registration process smoothly.

Step 1: Determine Eligibility Based on Turnover

Before initiating the GST registration process, it’s essential to assess your eligibility based on your annual turnover. The term “annual turnover” refers to the total value of your supplies, including taxable and non-taxable items. If your aggregate turnover exceeds the specified limit for your state or special category, GST registration becomes mandatory.

Step 2: Gather Required Documents

To initiate the registration process, you need to gather relevant documents, such as proof of address, proof of business address, and PAN-linked contact details. These documents are vital for establishing the authenticity of your business. Additionally, if you are a proprietor or a company, you may need your business registration certificate and books of accounts.

Step 3: Visit the GST Portal

Access the official GST portal and navigate to the registration section. Here, you will find a user-friendly interface where you can begin your GST registration application.

Step 4: Fill in Business Details

Enter your business details accurately in the registration application form. Make sure to provide information about your primary business location, the nature of your business, and your annual revenue.

Step 5: Upload Required Documents

Use the document upload feature on the portal to submit the scanned copies of the required documents. Ensure that the documents are legible and meet the specified criteria, such as file size and format.

Step 6: PAN and Aadhaar-based Authentication

For security and authentication purposes, the GST portal uses PAN and Aadhaar-based authentication. This step helps prevent fake registrations and ensures that the information provided is accurate.

Step 7: Verification and Submission

Review the information provided in the application form and attached documents. Once you are confident that all details are accurate, submit the application.

Step 8: Application Review and Processing

After submission, your application will undergo a verification process by the GST Department. In case there are any discrepancies or missing information, you might be contacted for clarification.

Step 9: Receive Certificate of Registration

Upon successful verification, you will receive your GST registration certificate. This certificate serves as proof of your registration as a taxable entity under GST.

Step 10: Post-Registration Formalities

Now that you are a registered taxpayer, it’s important to understand your responsibilities. You need to file regular GST returns, which involves reporting your taxable supplies, exempt supplies, and claiming Input Tax Credit. The GST portal provides a convenient platform for the filing of GST returns.

Who should obtain GST registration?

GST registration is necessary for the following individuals and entities:

  • Individuals who were registered under the Pre-GST law, such as Excise, VAT, Service Tax, etc.
  • Businesses with a turnover exceeding the threshold limit, which can be Rs. 40 lakh, Rs. 20 lakh, or Rs. 10 lakh, depending on the case.
  • Casual taxable persons and non-resident taxable persons.
  • Agents of a supplier and Input Service Distributors.
  • Those who pay tax under the reverse charge mechanism.
  • Individuals who supply goods or services through an e-commerce aggregator.
  • Every e-commerce aggregator itself.
  • Persons who provide online information and database access or retrieval services from a location outside India to a person in India, except registered taxable persons.

  • Address proof of the place of business
  • Bank Account statement/Cancelled cheque
  • Digital Signature
  • Letter of Authorization/Board Resolution for Authorised Signatory
